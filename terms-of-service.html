Synths makes no representation that the Reference Price reflects the true or fair value of the referenced Private Company, nor that the Market Price of any Synthetic Unit will converge with the Reference Price.

Article 7 – Trading and Market Conduct

7.1 Buying and Selling. Users may buy Synthetic Units at the prevailing Market Price and may sell Synthetic Units they hold at the prevailing Market Price. The Platform does not support short selling.

7.2 Order Types. The Platform currently supports market orders and limit orders. Market orders are executed at the best available Market Price at the time of execution. Limit orders are executed only at the User-specified price or better.

7.3 Prohibited Trading Conduct. The following trading conduct is strictly prohibited:

(a) Wash Trading: entering into trades that result in no genuine change in beneficial ownership for the purpose of creating artificial volume or a misleading appearance of market activity.
(b) Spoofing: submitting orders with the intent to cancel them prior to execution for the purpose of creating a false impression of supply or demand.
(c) Layering: placing multiple orders at different price levels without the intention of executing them to move the Market Price.
(d) Front-Running: trading on the basis of advance knowledge of pending orders from other Platform Users.
(e) Reference Price Manipulation: disseminating false or misleading information about a Private Company for the purpose of influencing the Reference Price or Market Price.
(f) Any other act constituting market manipulation under the Securities Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. S.5, or any successor legislation.

7.4 Circuit Breakers and Trading Halts. Synths employs LULD-style circuit breakers and confidence bands to maintain orderly markets. Trading in a Synthetic Unit may be halted automatically if the Market Price moves beyond defined thresholds or deviates materially from the Reference Price.
